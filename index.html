What is mental health?

The organization Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration writes about mental health. According to them, mental health is "our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act, and helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices."

Source:

samhsa.gov

Teenage mental health

Many things can negatively affect teens' mental health. According to the website healthychildren.org, three main things that have large effects are academic pressure, tough schedules, and generally difficult day-to-day problems.

Academic pressure: the overwhelming pressure to get good grades, pass every test, do every assignment well, impress colleges, and get academic validation.

Tough schedules: long days, few breaks, many responsibilities, extracurriculars, personal concerns, and lack of sleep.

Difficult day-to-day problems: family problems, friend problems, romantic problems, bullying, loneliness, depression, anxiety, and just life being really difficult.

Source:

healthychildren.org

How to help with mental health struggles

There are many ways to help with teens' mental health.

According to youth.gov, here are ways for schools to help teens' mental health:

  • develop programs to provide a positive school climate
  • develop services for students in need of additional support
  • develop programs that address the different mental health needs of their students
  • develop a school culture where teachers and staff can recognize mental health issues with students

What can you do to help your mental health?

  • make a social connection
  • stay active
  • talk to someone
  • take up a relaxation practice
  • don't cut back on sleep
  • reach out for help

What can you do to help a friend's mental health?

  • find out if the person is getting the care that they need - if not, help them get help
  • express your concern and support
  • ask questions, listen to ideas, be responsive when the topic of mental health comes up
  • offer to help with everyday tasks
  • reassure them that you care about them

How to talk about mental health:

  • "I've been worried about you. Can we talk?"
  • "What can I do to help?"
  • "What do you want me to know aobut how you are feeling?"
  • "Who or what has helped you deal with similar issues in the past?"
  • "How can I help you find help?"
